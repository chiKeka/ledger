How this works

  • Every claim enters with a stated probability, the test that resolves it, a deadline, real-world grounding, and provenance: bruno marks positions I have published or ratified, twin marks the system's own analyses. The two records are scored separately, so the machine cannot launder its guesses into my track record.
  • Probabilities lock at registration. Resolution requires the stated test to be met; partial and unresolvable are honest outcomes.
  • Forecasts are tagged worldview (inside my analytical frameworks) or world (events the frameworks do not route) — being calibrated at home and being calibrated are different claims, and both get measured.
  • Brier score: 0.000 is prophecy, 0.250 is coin-flipping. Skill is measured against the base rate recorded with each claim.
