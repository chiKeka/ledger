How to read this board
Probability
the large number on each card — the stated chance the claim comes true, locked the moment it was made.
Brier score
how accurate the calls are. Lower is better: 0 is perfect, a coin flip is 0.250.
Bruno & the twin
brass marks are Bruno's own calls; cobalt marks are an AI (“the twin”) forecasting separately. The two are scored apart, so the machine cannot launder its guesses into the human's record.
Structural vs calibration
structural claims are big bets on how the world works; calibration claims are small, fast forecasts that exist to tune the estimator, not to matter individually.
Seal
a resolved prediction, scored against what actually happened.
Voided
thrown out because the answer was already public before the bet was registered — kept on the board forever with its reason, but never counted as a win or a loss.
Frame: in / out
“in” fits Bruno's own frameworks; “out” is an event out in the world — a vote, a market move, a data release.

THE COVENANT

Method
  1. Every claim enters with a stated probability, a resolution test, a deadline, and a real-world grounding. The probability locks at registration and is never edited.
  2. Provenance is split: brass marks positions Bruno has published or ratified, cobalt marks the twin's own analyses. The two records are scored separately, so the machine cannot launder its guesses into the human's track record.
  3. Resolution requires the stated test to be met. Partial and unresolvable are honest outcomes, and the misses stay on the board.
  4. Every resolution is scored by Brier: 0.000 is prophecy, 0.250 is coin-flipping. The seal records the event, the call, and the sharpness separately, because being right and being calibrated are different achievements.
  5. Claims are framed worldview (inside the author's analytical map) or world (events the map does not route). Being calibrated at home and being calibrated are different claims; both are measured.
  6. Claims may be linked: a fast probe takes a reading on a slow position, a sharper claim restates a vaguer one, opposing claims cannot both fully hold. Links are structure, never score.
  7. A resolution whose outcome was publicly knowable before registration is invalidated: it stays on the board forever with its reason, excluded from the record, the Brier score, and calibration. Every new claim requires a pre-registration evidence search confirming its test has not already fired. The record does not keep wins it did not earn.
  8. Methodology experiments — retrodictions run to test the instrument against known history — are not forecasts about the world. They live in the workshop, off the live record entirely: never counted, never scored, never mixed into the board above.
