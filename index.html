THE COVENANT

Method
  1. Every claim enters with a stated probability, a resolution test, a deadline, and a real-world grounding. The probability locks at registration and is never edited.
  2. Provenance is split: brass marks positions Bruno has published or ratified, cobalt marks the twin's own analyses. The two records are scored separately, so the machine cannot launder its guesses into the human's track record.
  3. Resolution requires the stated test to be met. Partial and unresolvable are honest outcomes, and the misses stay on the board.
  4. Every resolution is scored by Brier: 0.000 is prophecy, 0.250 is coin-flipping. The seal records the event, the call, and the sharpness separately, because being right and being calibrated are different achievements.
  5. Claims are framed worldview (inside the author's analytical map) or world (events the map does not route). Being calibrated at home and being calibrated are different claims; both are measured.
  6. Claims may be linked: a fast probe takes a reading on a slow position, a sharper claim restates a vaguer one, opposing claims cannot both fully hold. Links are structure, never score.
